Li Ka Shing

  • Career
  • Philanthropy and legacy

Here's a time line of Sir Li Ka-shing career:

  • 1940 - Li and his family fled to Hong Kong refugees from the Sino-Japanese war.
  • 1950 - Li founded a plastic manufacturing company in Hong Kong with personal savings and funds borrowed from relatives.
  • 1958 - Li decided to purchase a site and develop his own factory building.
  • 1979 - Li purchased a major stake in Hutchison Whampoa Company Limited from Hong Kong Bank through Cheung Kong.
  • 2009/2019 - Li Ka-shing led a $15.5 million Series B round of financing for Siri Inc.
  • 2018 - Li announced his retirement and the decision to pass his $100 billion empire on to his son.

Here's a list of Sir Li Ka-shing philanthropies:

  • Li Ka-shing donated C$25 million to St. Michael's Hospital in Toronto to found the Li Ka-Shing Knowledge Institute, which will serve as a medical research and education centre in downtown Toronto.
  • To date, Li Ka-shing has invested over HK$30 billion in projects covering education, medical services, charity and anti-poverty programmes.
  • In September 2001, the newest tower in the Hong Kong Polytechnic University was named after Li, following a HK$100 million donation to the university.
  • Li Ka-shing donated C$28 million to the University of Alberta to found the Li Ka-shing Institute of Virology.
  • In 2005, Li announced a US$128 million donation to the Faculty of Medicine, University of Hong Kong.
  • 1979 - Li purchased a major stake in Hutchison Whampoa Company Limited from Hong Kong Bank through Cheung Kong.
  • Li Ka-shing donated US$20.6 million to aid relief efforts in the 2008 Sichuan earthquake.
